# Code of Conduct

> The behavior we expect of everyone who takes part in the CompassAlpha community.

CompassAlpha is built in the open, and the quality of the framework depends on the quality of the conversation around it. This code of conduct applies to all project spaces — issues, discussions, pull requests, and any official channel — and to anyone who participates in them.

## Our intent

We want a community where people can disagree about ideas sharply and still treat each other well. Rigorous critique of doctrine, designs, and documentation is the whole point. Hostility toward people is never part of it.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and assume good faith. Most disagreements are about the work, not the person.
- Critique the idea, not the contributor. "This axiom contradicts §3" is useful; insults are not.
- Be clear and specific. Cite the page, line, or claim you are responding to.
- Welcome newcomers and questions, including ones that seem basic.
- Accept feedback gracefully, and give it the same way you would want to receive it.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks of any kind.
- Discriminatory, demeaning, or hateful language or imagery, including that targeting any protected characteristic.
- Sexual attention or advances that are unwelcome.
- Publishing others' private information without their explicit consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, deliberate derailing, or bad-faith argument.
- Encouraging or condoning any of the above.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for upholding this code and may take any action they judge appropriate — including editing or removing contributions, and warning, suspending, or banning a participant — in response to behavior that violates it.

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to **conduct@gradus.pk**. Reports are handled discreetly. We will review every report in good faith and respect the privacy and safety of the reporter.

## Scope

This code applies in all project spaces and when an individual is representing the project in public. Maintainers who do not uphold it in good faith may face consequences determined by the project's stewards.
